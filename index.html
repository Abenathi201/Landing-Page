CM Punk

Brooks has used the CM Punk moniker for his entire professional wrestling career, and his character has been consistently portrayed as outspoken, confrontational, sharp-tongued, anti-establishment, straight edge, and iconoclastic, most of which are inspired by his real-life views and personality.

Roddy Piper

Piper earned the nicknames "Rowdy" and "Hot Rod" by displaying his trademark "Scottish" short temper, spontaneity, and quick wit. According to The Daily Telegraph, he is "considered by many to be the greatest 'heel' (or villain) wrestler ever".

Steve Austin

He is known for portraying a disrespectful, beer-drinking antihero and had a popular feud with his boss Mr. McMahon during The Attitude Era. His finishing move is the Stone Cold Stunner. He was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame in 2009 by Vince McMahon.

Professional wrestling, often simply referred to as wrestling, is a form of scripted entertainment that combines elements of sports, theater, and storytelling. It involves highly trained performers, known as professional wrestlers or simply wrestlers, engaging in choreographed matches to entertain the audience. While the outcomes are predetermined and the matches are scripted, the physicality and athleticism displayed by the wrestlers are real, and they perform their stunts and moves with precision and skill. Key Components of Professional Wrestling: Storylines and Characters: One of the primary aspects of professional wrestling is the creation of engaging storylines and colorful characters. Wrestlers are often portrayed as heroes (faces) or villains (heels) and have distinct personas with unique traits, catchphrases, and costumes. These characters form the basis of the drama and intrigue that unfolds in the wrestling world. Promotions: Professional wrestling is organized and promoted by companies known as promotions. These promotions are responsible for creating events, managing storylines, hiring and training wrestlers, and marketing their shows to attract audiences. Some well-known wrestling promotions include WWE (World Wrestling Entertainment), AEW (All Elite Wrestling), NJPW (New Japan Pro-Wrestling), and Impact Wrestling. Matches: Wrestling matches are the central focus of the events. Matches take place in a wrestling ring, which is typically a raised platform surrounded by ropes. Wrestlers compete against each other using a combination of strikes, holds, and high-impact moves to win the match. The objective of a match can vary, but the most common way to win is by pinning your opponent's shoulders to the mat for a three-count, forcing them to submit, or winning by disqualification. Feuds and Rivalries: Feuds and rivalries are storylines built around the conflicts between wrestlers or groups of wrestlers. These storylines create dramatic tension and are usually resolved through matches at special events called pay-per-views. Feuds can last for weeks or even months, providing ongoing entertainment and suspense for fans. Pay-Per-Views: Pay-per-views (PPVs) are special events where fans must pay to watch the matches on television or online. These events feature high-profile matches, often culminating in the resolution of major storylines and rivalries. WrestleMania, held annually by WWE, is one of the most famous and significant pay-per-views in professional wrestling. Promos: Promos, short for "promotional interviews," are segments where wrestlers speak directly to the audience or engage in verbal confrontations with their rivals. Wrestlers use promos to advance storylines, build hype for upcoming matches, and showcase their mic skills and charisma. Safety: Despite the scripted nature of professional wrestling, safety is a top priority. Wrestlers undergo rigorous training to learn how to execute moves safely and protect themselves and their opponents from injury. They communicate during matches to ensure that complex maneuvers are executed properly and without harm. Professional wrestling has a massive global following, with millions of fans attending live events and even more watching broadcasts on television and streaming platforms. It continues to evolve, blending traditional wrestling elements with modern storytelling techniques, making it an enduring and influential form of entertainment worldwide.
